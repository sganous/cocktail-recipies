Bee's Knees

Alternate twist on a sour cocktail.

A very sweet and refreshing drink for easy sipping on a summer day.
This drink is thought to have come up during the prohibition period of the United States.
During those times, there was a lot of questionable liquor roaming the shelves, so this
drink was created to help mask some of the unpleasantries that could arise, coining the ironic
name of the "Bee's Knees". Regarless of intent, the floral pallet of gin pairs wonderfully with
lemon and honey.

Recipe

  • 2 ounces of gin
  • 3/4 ounce lemon juice
  • 3/4 ounce honey syrup
  • Lemon peel for garnish

Steps

  1. Add gin, lemon juice, and honey to a cocktail shaker.
  2. Add ice and shake until chilled.
  3. Strain into a chilled couple or cocktail glass.
  4. Garnish with a lemon peel.
